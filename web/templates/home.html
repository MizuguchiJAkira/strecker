How it works

Four nights a season. The rest runs itself.

You already run cameras. Strecker is the part that happens after the card comes back to the truck — sorting, counting, and keeping the record so you can actually use it next year.

01 / Pull the card

Drop the photos in

SD card, phone, drag-and-drop — any camera brand. EXIF timestamps and station come along with the photos.

02 / The sort

Every frame, by species

Two trained detectors (MegaDetector + SpeciesNet) read every photo. Triggers from the same animal inside 30 minutes collapse into one event, so a buck standing at the feeder doesn't count as forty deer.

03 / The record

A logbook you'll actually open

Species list. Activity by hour. Per-station tallies. Photos indexed so you can pull up every bear sighting at Crooked Well in October with one click.

On the way

What we’re building next.

The core logbook — species inventory, event counts, per-camera activity, photo gallery, density with confidence intervals — works today on your card. Here’s what’s in flight for the beta.

Next up / re-id

Name every buck.

Individual re-identification — each deer embedded with a trained encoder, matched against the roster of animals on your property. First-seen, last-seen, and a running history per buck.

Next up / antlers

Buck-to-doe ratio, honestly.

Antler classification active May through November, during hard-antler months. Disabled until the classifier is trained and evaluated — we won’t show randomized ratios as if they were real.

Next up / trends

What changed since last season.

Per-species detection rate, hourly activity shift, and population trajectory across seasons. Unlocks automatically when you have two seasons of data uploaded.

Next up / corrections

Fix a misclassification in one click.

See a bobcat labeled as a gray fox? Click to correct. Your feedback feeds a per-region accuracy tracker and tightens the model for every card after yours.

Next up / mobile

Upload from the truck.

Direct SD-card ingest from the phone, push notifications when processing finishes, offline review of recent sightings. Web works now. Native app is next.

Next up / teams

Bring the whole crew.

Co-hunters, ranch managers, and leaseholders on the same property with scoped permissions. One upload, many eyes on the same logbook.
