What the journal keeps
i.

A species inventory.

MegaDetector and SpeciesNet read every photo; independent-event rules collapse bursts; the result is a season-level list of every animal that used your land and how often.

ii.

A density estimate, with a confidence interval.

The Random Encounter Model (Rowcliffe 2008) converts detections into animals per square kilometre; a bootstrap over 1,000 resamples gives you the 95% CI, not a point guess.

iii.

An activity pattern, by hour.

Hourly event counts per species. You see at a glance when your deer move, when your hogs feed, when the cats are active — and whether that shifts across the season.

Methods

Every number Strecker publishes is traceable back to a specific photograph. Detection is by MegaDetector v5; species assignment by SpeciesNet v4.0.2, calibrated to an 8% review threshold. Independent events are filtered to a 30-minute window per camera per species. Density follows the Random Encounter Model. Placement bias (feeder, trail, water attractors) is corrected via inverse-propensity weighting on literature-prior inflation factors.

The record is meant to stand alongside a wildlife biologist's field notes, not replace them. If the data disagrees with the land, believe the land. — notes on method
