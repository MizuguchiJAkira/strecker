Methodology
click to expand ↓ click to collapse ↑
What this report measures

Three pipeline outputs, in order of methodological primacy: detection frequency (events per camera-day, the raw relative-abundance index); density (animals/km², via the Random Encounter Model); tier (Low / Moderate / Elevated / Severe, feral-hog only at v1, per Mayer & Brisbin 2009). The composite Exposure Score (0–100) is a piecewise-linear transform of density anchored on the tier cutoffs. The modeled annual-damage projection is not a pipeline output — it's scaled from Anderson 2016 per-hog figures × area × crop modifier and labeled MODELED PROJECTION throughout.

Density estimator (REM)

D = (y / t) × π / (v × r × (2 + θ))
where y/t is detections per camera-day, v is species-specific mean daily travel distance (hog 6.0 km/day — Kay 2017; white-tailed deer 1.5 km/day — Webb 2010; coyote 10.0 km/day — Andelt 1985), r is the camera detection radius (0.015 km), θ is the camera detection angle (0.7 rad). This report used {{ stats.total_events }} independent detections across {{ stats.n_cameras }} cameras over {{ stats.season_days }} survey days. Confidence intervals via 1,000-iteration nonparametric bootstrap over cameras with truncated-normal perturbation of v.

Placement-bias correction

Kolowski & Forrester (2017) document 1.4–9.7× detection inflation at non-random camera placements vs. random. The pipeline deflates per-camera rates by a per-species, per-context inflation factor (feral hog: feeder 10×, food_plot 6×, trail 4×, water 3×, random 1×, other 1.5×), then averages across cameras. A second Hájek IPW estimator (Cassel-Särndal-Wretman 1976; Hájek 1971) with empirical propensities is reported alongside as a diagnostic. Diagnostic caveats fire automatically when effective sample size (Kish 1965) drops below n/2, the maximum- weight ratio exceeds 5× (Cole & Hernán 2008), or no random- placement cameras anchor the deployment.

Recommendation flags

insufficient_data ← < 100 camera-days OR < 20 events. recommend_supplementary_survey ← CI upper / lower ratio > 1.5. sufficient_for_decision ← otherwise.

Limitations & out-of-scope

Detection frequency is a relative-abundance index; absolute density via REM rests on the daily-distance values and detection-cone parameters listed above, both carrying inter- region uncertainty not fully captured by the bootstrap CI. Damage projections are model-based, not observed losses. Tier classification is defined for feral hog only at v1; other species appear with density only.
